Opening Remarks

~50 seconds — after the welcome / hand-off

Script Deliver conversationally — feel free to riff
Look around the table — there is probably more deployable climate and infrastructure capital represented in this room than in any single conversation you’ve been in this year. [beat]
We have the people writing the largest checks: Majid and Karim from Alterra, the $30B vehicle that came out of COP28; Richard from Mubadala; and David from Morgan Stanley, who sits on top of one of the deepest pools of institutional capital in the world.
We have the platform builders — Marc Ganzi, who has become the loudest honest voice on the AI infrastructure capex super-cycle; Bob Morse at Bridge; Ted Koenig, who has watched private credit step into the financing gap the banks left behind.
We have the early-stage and the European climate-tech end of this — Mahmoud from Shorooq, Christian from 2150 — building the supply of companies the rest of you will eventually buy or back.
We have Sachit at Tillman, who is actually putting steel in the ground in the markets the climate narrative talks about and rarely funds.
And we have Brooke, Albert, and Kimberly — family capital, operator capital, and the deal lawyer who sees most of this flow before any of us get to read about it. [beat]
So: a lot of capital, a lot of vantage points, and an honest disagreement waiting to surface about how much of this is real, how much is theater, and where the next dislocation actually shows up. Let’s get into it.
Tip: the [beat] pauses are good moments to make eye contact around the table and let the room settle before you move into Q1.

Interesting Pairings & Tensions

Al Suwaidi / Radwan ↔ Nordell
Two flagship UAE-anchored capital pools sitting side by side. Different mandates — Alterra is climate-thematic and EM-tilted, Mubadala is generalist and global. Useful to draw out where they overlap, where they don’t, and where Gulf capital is actually crowding into the same trades.
Ganzi ↔ Ahuja
Both digital infrastructure operators. Ganzi at OECD scale and on the AI capex front line; Ahuja in the harder, less-funded EM markets. Same thesis, very different unit economics.
Heaton ↔ Koenig
Asset manager vs. private credit specialist. Where the largest LPs are placing the asset class, vs. how the actual lending is being underwritten. Natural debate on private-credit risk pricing.
Adi ↔ Jølck
Both early-stage climate & tech investors, but in radically different ecosystems — MENA vs. Northern Europe. Use for a counterpoint on where founders are coming from and what the supply of investable companies actually looks like.
Hill ↔ Morse
Both close to the gritty real-asset and energy-transition end of the trade, at very different scales. Useful to surface where institutional capital is overpaying and where mid-market discipline is still doing the underwriting work.
Petillo-Decossard ↔ Everyone
She sees the friction. Anytime the conversation goes too deal-romantic, pull her in for what is actually killing transactions: tariffs, sanctions, CFIUS, antitrust, sponsor diligence.
Milstein ↔ the fund managers
Family capital with a different clock vs. funds running on a 5- to 7-year cycle. Good reset when the conversation drifts into IRR-speak and away from real assets.

Option A: “Capital, Constraint, Climate”

Follows the capital stack from sovereign down to operator, ending on what is actually getting built.

Opening · ~15 min · Where is the capital actually going?
Heaton frames the institutional flows → Al Suwaidi on Alterra deployment vs. ambition → Nordell on Mubadala’s seat across the stack → Koenig on private credit’s share of the build-out
Act II · ~25 min · The constraints — physical, political, structural
Ganzi on power, land, permitting → Ahuja on the EM grid and demand-curve reality → Morse & Hill on mid-market real-asset and industrial bottlenecks → Petillo-Decossard on the deal-table friction (tariffs, CFIUS, sanctions)
Act III · ~20 min · Where is the next opportunity — and the next dislocation?
Jølck on the European climate-tech supply → Adi on MENA founders → Radwan on what Alterra will actually fund next → Milstein on long-duration view → Close with: what does this room get wrong?

Option B: “Real vs. Theater”

Higher-energy structure. Surfaces the gap between climate-and-AI narratives and what is actually getting underwritten.

Opening · ~15 min · The honest state of the trade
Provocation: a lot of capital has been raised; how much has actually been deployed at the prices and on the terms originally pitched? → Heaton on the LP read → Koenig on what the credit side is seeing → Ganzi on which pieces of digital infrastructure are real vs. priced for perfection
Act II · ~25 min · Where is the value, where is the theater?
Hill & Morse on industrial and mid-market reality → Al Suwaidi & Radwan on Alterra’s deal funnel (what is actually bankable?) → Ahuja on EM digital infrastructure economics → Jølck on the built environment as the largest underfunded climate trade → Adi on what regional founders see that the LPs don’t
Act III · ~20 min · Friction, governance, what breaks first
Petillo-Decossard on what is actually killing deals → Nordell on sovereign capital’s real role → Milstein on what permanent capital wants → Close on: 12 months from now, where does the dislocation come from — AI capex, private credit, climate underwriting, or geopolitics?

Back-Pocket Questions

For awkward pauses, one-word answers, or when a topic needs a reset.

If you had to bet your own money — not your fund’s — on the single most durable infrastructure or climate trade for the next five years, where does it go?
Who in this room is most likely to be proven right on the AI infrastructure build-out — and who is most likely to be proven wrong?
What’s the question about your own portfolio that you’re afraid to ask out loud in your IC?
Has anyone here changed their mind about something significant in the last six months — on climate, on infrastructure, on the Gulf, on private credit?
If the next dislocation comes from this part of the market, what’s the tell? What are you watching now that no one else seems to be?
Where is climate capital being deployed honestly, and where is it greenwash dressed up as an asset class?
A meaningful share of the capital around this table is Gulf-anchored. Is the Gulf becoming the marginal price-setter for global infrastructure — and if so, what does that change?
👉 [To someone quiet] You’ve been listening. What’s landing wrong for you in this conversation?
